I'm Rajesh (RJ).

I've spent my whole career on one question: how do you make a system dependable enough to trust it in production — running on its own, where being wrong has real consequences? I keep chasing that question into harder places, each with a bigger blast radius than the last.

It started with systems from first principles. As a student I wrote an operating system from scratch — a microkernel with its own scheduler, IPC, and memory manager (ManriX) — because I wanted to understand how a computer really works, all the way down. I never lost that instinct: I'd rather understand the bottom of the stack than the top of the hype.

That became dependable platforms at scale: an IoT SaaS connecting millions of devices, a financial-grade PaaS where downtime was not acceptable, and a data streaming platform where I worked on control planes for Kafka across thousands of clusters — plus a secure runtime for trusted and untrusted workloads on shared infrastructure.

Now I'm working on safe production action: how does an AI agent do more than diagnose? How does it act, with every step scoped, approved, audited, and reversible?

Intent should never reach production unchecked. Validate it. Gate the dangerous parts. Make it reversible. Whether it's an operator reconciling a cluster or an AI fixing an incident — that's the shape of every system I trust.
